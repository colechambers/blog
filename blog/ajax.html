Technology continues to have a profound effect on the growth of our civilization. Specifically web technologies have grown exponentially and have changed the way we interact with other people, the way we complete research, and the way we consume media, to name just a few.

It's hard for me to remember the time before the Internet, though I certainly was alive for it. I used to have to go to a library and use a card based system for finding books. I used to have to call a travel agent for plane tickets. And to check the weather, well you could read the newspaper or watch the evening news. Now for all of these tasks I can complete them online in fraction of the time, whenever I want. Information technology has increased our productivity significantly.

My drive for wanting to learn web technologies stems from my desire to harness the productive, creative, and monetary rewards that comes from utilizing technology. Learning to code has helped me to see the world differently, and has helped me to become better at solving problems. It also allows me to express myself and make an impact.

Currently I am learning HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, node.js, MongoDB, AngularJS, jQuery, and other emerging web technologies. I believe this is an awesome time to be a web developer as many of these technologies have matured and provide an excellent foundation to build upon.

As I look to the future, I see the impact of the Internet continuing to grow, as we become more inter-connected and more data dependent. The possibilities are limitless, and I look forward to years of exciting work as I continue my journey to gain mastery of this technology.

Cole Chambers is a web developer based out of Boulder, CO. He is currently a full-stack student in the immersive web development program at Galvanize.
